About Student's POV

Engineering help from a student's point of view

Hi, everyone! My name is Alex, and I am a rising junior studying electrical engineering at Southern Methodist University. I made this website to share helpful resources with STEM students who are working through tough classes, confusing problems, and topics that do not always click the first time. Sometimes a lecture or textbook explanation can feel too advanced too quickly, so Student's POV is meant to slow things down and explain ideas from the perspective of someone who has had to struggle through the material too.

What this site is about

Student's POV combines tutoring, engineering projects, and documentation so learners can see both the polished answer and the thinking process that gets there.

Why I Started

Learning feels different when the steps are not skipped

I started Student's POV because I know what it feels like to sit with a difficult math or engineering concept and feel like every explanation skips the part where it actually starts to make sense. This site is my way of building the kind of resource I wish I had: clear, step-by-step lessons from someone still close enough to the learning process to remember where students get stuck.

My Mission

Make technical subjects feel less intimidating

My mission is to make technical subjects feel less intimidating by explaining not only the answer, but the reasoning behind it. I want students to see the steps, the patterns, the mistakes, and the intuition that turn a confusing problem into something they can actually work through.

My Background

Engineering student, builder, and note keeper

I am an electrical engineering student at Southern Methodist University, building projects, tutorials, and study tools while developing my skills in circuits, programming, physics, and problem solving. Student's POV is both a tutoring resource and a personal archive, a place where I can document how to work through core problems so I do not forget the methods, patterns, and intuition behind them.
